The MIA (Myco Investigation Agency) project aims to create a comprehensive fungal collection (mycotheca) to support the exploration of the still largely unknown fungal world. By isolating and preserving diverse fungal strains, the project seeks to expand our scientific understanding of fungal biodiversity. As part of the initiative, selected isolates will be tested as biofertilizers to promote the growth of dwarf tomato plants. A key objective of MIA is also to ensure that the collected material is accessible and shareable, fostering collaboration and future research in fungal biotechnology and sustainable agriculture.
